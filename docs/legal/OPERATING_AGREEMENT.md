## 4. MANAGEMENT AND OPERATION

### 4.1 Member-Managed Company
The Company shall be managed by the Member (member-managed). The Member shall have the power to manage the business, operations, and affairs of the Company, including but not limited to the power to make decisions regarding:

(a) Business operations and strategy
(b) Capital expenditures and investments
(c) Hiring, compensation, and termination of employees and contractors
(d) Banking relationships and credit arrangements
(e) Lease agreements and real property transactions
(f) Intellectual property development and licensing
(g) Entry into contracts and agreements

### 4.2 Authority of Member
The Member shall have full power and authority to execute contracts, agreements, and documents on behalf of the Company and to bind the Company to such contracts and agreements.

### 4.3 Meetings
There shall be no formal meetings required under this Agreement. The Member may act at any time without notice or formality. The Member may document any decisions in writing in the form of a meeting memorandum or written resolution.

### 4.4 Banking Authority
The Member is authorized to:

(a) Open, maintain, and close bank accounts in the Company's name
(b) Deposit Company funds into designated accounts
(c) Withdraw Company funds and make disbursements
(d) Execute banking documents, checks, and electronic funds transfers
(e) Establish lines of credit and borrowing arrangements

### 4.5 Books and Records
The Member shall maintain complete and accurate books of account and records of the Company, including:

(a) Financial statements and accounting records
(b) Member capital accounts
(c) Tax returns and documentation
(d) Records of distributions
(e) Contracts and agreements
(f) Intellectual property documentation

## 5. CAPITAL ACCOUNTS AND DISTRIBUTIONS

### 5.1 Capital Account
A capital account shall be established and maintained for the Member showing:

(a) The Member's original capital contributions
(b) Increases or decreases in the Member's capital account
(c) Allocations of profits and losses
(d) Distributions to the Member

### 5.2 Profit and Loss Allocation
All net profits, net losses, and all items of income, gain, loss, deduction, and credit of the Company for each fiscal year shall be allocated 100% to the Member.

### 5.3 Distributions
The Member shall be entitled to distributions of Company profits as determined by the Member in its sole discretion. Distributions may be made at any time and in such amounts as the Member deems appropriate.

### 5.4 Tax Distributions
To the extent practicable, the Company shall make distributions to the Member in amounts sufficient to permit the Member to pay income taxes with respect to the Member's share of Company income. The timing and amount of such distributions shall be at the Member's sole discretion.

### 5.5 No Distributions on Withdrawal
Upon the Member's withdrawal or the dissolution of the Company, distributions shall be made only after all liabilities and obligations of the Company have been satisfied.

### 5.6 Distributions in Kind
Distributions may be made in cash, Company property, or a combination thereof, as determined by the Member.

## 6B. TECHNOLOGY AND AI OPERATIONS

### 6B.1 AI Systems
The Company develops and operates artificial intelligence systems as part of its software products, including but not limited to AI-powered analytical tools ("AI Coach") that process user data to generate analytical insights. The Member acknowledges that:

(a) AI systems may utilize third-party AI service providers (including but not limited to OpenAI) for processing;
(b) The Company is responsible for ensuring compliance with applicable data privacy and consumer protection laws in connection with AI operations;
(c) AI outputs generated by the Company's products do not constitute investment advice, financial recommendations, or professional guidance of any kind.

### 6B.2 Data Processing and Privacy
The Company processes, stores, and transmits personal data and financial/trading data of its users. The Member shall ensure that:

(a) The Company maintains appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect user data;
(b) The Company complies with applicable data protection laws, including but not limited to the Florida Information Protection Act (Fla. Stat. § 501.171), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) where applicable;
(c) The Company maintains and publishes a Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, and other legally required disclosures;
(d) Data breach response procedures are documented and the Member has sole authority to make decisions regarding breach notification and response.

### 6B.3 Data Breach Response Authority
In the event of a data breach or suspected data breach involving Company systems or user data, the Member shall have sole authority and responsibility to:

(a) Determine the scope and severity of the breach;
(b) Engage legal counsel, forensic investigators, and other advisors as necessary;
(c) Make all decisions regarding breach notification to affected individuals, regulatory authorities, and law enforcement;
(d) Authorize any public statements or disclosures regarding the breach;
(e) Approve expenditures related to breach response and remediation.

## 7. DISSOLUTION AND LIQUIDATION

### 7.1 Dissolution Events
The Company shall be dissolved upon the earliest of:

(a) The occurrence of an event of dissolution specified in the Act;
(b) The passage of a definite term of years (if any such term has been specified in the Articles of Organization);
(c) The death or incapacity of the sole Member, unless the Company is continued under the Act;
(d) Written dissolution decision by the Member;
(e) Judicial dissolution under the Act.

### 7.2 Wind-Up and Liquidation
Upon dissolution of the Company, the Member (or a designated liquidator) shall wind up the affairs of the Company and liquidate its assets. The Company shall:

(a) Cease new business operations
(b) Collect all Company assets
(c) Pay all debts and liabilities
(d) Return all property of third parties
(e) Distribute remaining assets to the Member according to the Member's capital account

### 7.3 Succession Upon Death
If the sole Member dies, the Company shall not be automatically dissolved. The Member's estate or designated successor may continue the Company's operations, assuming all rights and obligations under this Agreement. The successor shall become the new Member.

## 8. TRANSFER OF MEMBERSHIP INTERESTS

### 8.1 Restrictions on Transfer
The Member's membership interest in the Company may not be transferred, sold, pledged, hypothecated, or encumbered without the prior written approval of the remaining members (if any). Since there is only one member, any transfer must be documented and consented to by that member.

### 8.2 Right of First Refusal
If the Member desires to transfer any portion of the Member's interest, the remaining member shall have a right of first refusal to purchase such interest on the same terms offered to any prospective purchaser.

### 8.3 Assignment by Will
The Member may designate a successor to receive the Member's membership interest upon the Member's death by will or other testamentary instrument.

### 8.4 No Admission of Assignee
Any assignee of a membership interest shall not become a member and shall not have any rights as a member unless:

(a) Expressly approved by the remaining members; and
(b) The assignee executes a counterpart of this Agreement or a written acknowledgment of membership.
